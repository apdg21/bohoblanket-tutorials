Master the Finish: How to Box Cushion Corners Like a Pro!

Elevate your sewing projects with crisp, professional boxed corners that add structure and polish to any project.

Jump to Tutorial

Why Boxed Corners Make All the Difference

Boxed corners are a game-changing sewing technique that transforms flat projects into dimensional masterpieces. Unlike rounded or pointed corners, boxed corners create structure and give your work a professional, store-bought quality.

Perfect for:

  • Throw pillows and cushions
  • Tote bags and purses
  • Pet beds and floor pillows
  • Storage bins and organizers

Benefits:

  • Creates professional-looking structure
  • Prevents saggy, floppy corners
  • Adds dimension to flat projects
  • Makes items stand upright
Designer Tip: The size of your box determines the depth of your corner. Smaller boxes (1-1.5") work well for bags, while larger boxes (2-3") create dramatic dimension for floor pillows.

Step-by-Step Tutorial

Follow these simple steps to achieve perfect boxed corners every time. We'll use a cushion cover as our example, but the technique works for any project!

1 Prepare Your Project

Start with your sewn cushion cover inside out. All seams should be complete except for your turning/ stuffing opening. Lay the project flat and align the side and bottom seams perfectly at the corner you'll be boxing.

Key Point: Ensure your seams are pressed flat before beginning for the most accurate results.

2 Measure and Mark

Pinch the corner so side and bottom seams align perfectly, forming a triangle. From the tip, measure inward along the seam (this determines your box depth). Mark a straight line perpendicular to the seam.

Measurement Guide:
• Small bags: 1-1.5" (2.5-3.8cm)
• Standard cushions: 1.5-2" (3.8-5cm)
• Floor pillows: 2-3" (5-7.5cm)

3 Stitch and Trim

Sew along your marked line with a 1/4" seam allowance. Backstitch at both ends. Trim the excess fabric, leaving about 1/4" seam allowance. For heavy fabrics, grade the seams by trimming one layer shorter than the other.

Pro Finish: Notch the corner (cut a small triangle out of the seam allowance) to reduce bulk and help the corner lay flat when turned.

4 The Big Reveal!

Turn your project right side out. Gently push out the corners using a point turner or blunt tool. Press thoroughly with an iron for crisp edges. Admire your professional-looking boxed corners!

Troubleshooting: If corners look puckered, try clipping the seam allowance closer to the stitching or pressing more thoroughly with steam.
